Week 02 · 9 March

The tip of the iceberg

on perception, neuroplasticity, and the architecture of the mind

The idea that vision is just a brain interface, not a real window onto the world — that kind of thing makes people start wondering if everything's fake.

I see it a little differently. I don't think the world is virtual or fake. The weird sense of unreality people feel? It's from the opposite direction. Our virtual creations. Way too real now. Not that reality is unconvincing. The line between realistic digital experiences and actual physical existence has blurred. Our brains struggle to tell these layers of ‘truth’ apart.

Everything we perceive and understand is basically a result of mental processing. We never get the objective world in its full form. We only see the tip of a giant iceberg. — the architecture beneath the architecture

Makes me think about how powerful the mind really is. That's probably why placebos work so well, I think. A shift in belief or mental state can really change your body, if the mind is the filter all reality passes through. Your internal mind is what really makes the universe you perceive.

We also talked about how the environment rewires us. Neuroplasticity. Like how phantom limb pain hangs around because the brain's internal map hasn't caught up. A lot of people criticise modern digital environments for making us shallow or impatient. But being affected by our environment is just part of being human. It's not automatically bad. The challenge for someone living now is keeping a kind of careful thinking. You have to ask whether the trends and environments you're adapting to are actually taking you somewhere good. Adapting without thinking critically is just blind conformity.
