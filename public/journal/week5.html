Week 05 · 30 March

ACMI — gears to worlds

field notes from the moving image

After our exploration of early cinema, Week 5 was a field trip to ACMI, which kind of perfectly materialised everything from the lectures. Seeing the actual physical artefacts of early media was really cool — vintage dual-lens cameras, big 8mm film projectors with their massive reels, hand-cranked viewmasters where the inside pages flip really fast to create the illusion of motion (like a flip clock).

What hit me the most was how the museum displayed the evolution of visual storytelling. Past the vinyl records and mechanical miniatures, I saw what felt like the modern equivalents of those early film strips: the design manuscripts for The Lego Movie (the exact ones that bring the ‘Everything is Awesome’ song to mind), and the concept art for the video game Cult of the Lamb. It made me realise that whether it's a hand-cranked gear from the nineteenth century or a digital game sketch today, the human urge to build imaginary worlds is basically the same.
